Welcome to

COLUMBUS, INDIANA

A Modernist Architecture Destination

Known as a hidden gem for architecture enthusiasts,

Columbus, Indiana, is home to an extraordinary collection of modernist landmarks. Celebrated for its cutting-edge design and architectural innovation, Columbus offers visitors a unique glimpse into the works of some of the world’s most influential architects. Explore iconic buildings, take guided tours, and experience the harmony of art and functionality that makes this town a must-see destination for lovers of modern architecture.

TOP MODERNIST ARCHITECTURE LANDMARKS IN COLUMBUS, INDIANA

First Christian Church

Designed by: Eliel Saarinen (1942)

First Christian Church is a masterpiece of modernist design. With its geometric forms, clean lines, and 160-foot-high tower, this church is one of the earliest examples of modern architecture applied to a religious building in the U.S.

Location: 531 5th St, Columbus, IN 47201

Visiting Hours: Open to the public Monday through Friday, with self-guided tours available.

Travel Tip: Visit early in the morning for the best natural lighting to see the contrast of shadow and light on the structure.

Irwin Union Bank and Trust

Designed by: Eero Saarinen (1954)

This architectural triumph features a transparent, open design that breaks away from traditional bank architecture. Its modernist style invites natural light in, making the space feel open and welcoming.

Location: 500 Washington St, Columbus, IN 47201

Best Time to Visit: Daytime during banking hours, so you can see the interior design and experience the building in use.

Cleo Rogers Memorial Library

Designed by: I.M. Pei (1969)

The Cleo Rogers Memorial Library stands as a symbol of intellectual and community development. The open plaza, minimalist design, and red-brick construction create a warm yet striking appearance.

Location: 536 5th St, Columbus, IN 47201

Visiting Information: Open to the public. Special architectural tours are offered on weekends.

Highlight: Don’t miss the Henry Moore sculpture located in the plaza just outside the library.

Cummins Corporate Office Building

Designed by: Kevin Roche (1983)

Showcasing how modern architecture can blend with nature, this building’s use of glass and metal, combined with serene courtyards, gives it a sleek yet peaceful atmosphere.

Location: 500 Jackson St, Columbus, IN 47201

Travel Tip: Book a guided architecture tour that includes access to the interior.

North Christian Church

Designed by: Eero Saarinen (1964)

Known for its hexagonal shape and a 192-foot spire, this church is one of the last projects completed by Saarinen and is an iconic example of modernist religious architecture.

Location: 850 Tipton Ln, Columbus, IN 47203

Visiting Information: Open to the public for services and select tours.

Travel Tip: Visit during the late afternoon to capture stunning light hitting the spire.

Lincoln Elementary School

Designed by: Gunnar Birkerts (1967)

This modernist school emphasizes open, functional layouts and a connection to the outdoors.

Location: 750 5th St, Columbus, IN 47201

Visiting Information: Not generally open to the public, but the exterior can be viewed

Pro Tip: Visit during school hours to see the building in use and appreciate how its design facilitates learning.

Columbus City Hall

Designed by: Edward Charles Bassett (1981)

A modernist civic building that reflects the clean-line aesthetics of Columbus.

Location: 123 Washington St, Columbus, IN 47201

Visiting Information: Open to the public. Guided tours are available on select dates.

Highlight: Take a walk around the City Hall plaza to enjoy its minimalist landscaping.

St. Peter's Lutheran Church

Designed by: Gunnar Birkerts (1988)

A modernist church with a striking glass and brick design, offering large interior spaces that flood with natural light.

Location: 719 5th St, Columbus, IN 47201

Visiting Information: Open to the public for services and architectural tours.

Travel Tip: Attend a Sunday service to experience the building as a functional space.

Clifty Creek Elementary School

Designed by: Richard Meier (1981)

Meier’s design for this school emphasizes clean white lines and open spaces, characteristic of his modernist style.

Location: 4625 E 50 N, Columbus, IN 47203

Visiting Information: Not typically open to the public, but the exterior is worth a visit.

Pro Tip: Snap a photo of the sleek white facade in the afternoon light for the best contrast

The Republic Newspaper Building

Designed by: Myron Goldsmith (1971)

This modernist structure, known for its open-plan glass walls, has been celebrated as a National Historic Landmark due to its unique design.

Location: 333 2nd St, Columbus, IN 47201

Visiting Information: The building is in use, but exterior views are accessible to the public.

Highlight: Visit in the morning to capture the best reflections from the glass walls.

SUGGESTED ACTIVITIES FOR ARCHITECTURE ENTHUSIASTS

Architecture Walking Tours:

Explore the modernist landmarks with a guided walking tour organized by the Columbus Area Visitors Center. These tours provide in-depth insights into the architectural innovations that have made Columbus famous.

Tour Availability: Daily at 10 AM and 2 PM

Self-Guided Bike Tour:

For those who enjoy a little more independence, rent a bike and embark on a self-guided tour around the city. You can follow the Columbus Architecture Bike Trail, which passes by several key landmarks like the Miller House and Bartholomew County Library.

Bike Rentals: Available at various local shops. Check out the Columbus Area Visitors Center for trail maps.

Architectural Workshops:

The Columbus Visitors Center occasionally offers workshops for architecture enthusiasts, focusing on modernist design, structural innovation, and landscape architecture. These workshops provide a deeper understanding of how these concepts were applied in Columbus.

Art & Architecture Tour Combo:

Combine your love for architecture and art with a combo tour that includes visits to galleries featuring contemporary pieces alongside Columbus's architectural treasures.

Highlight: This tour often includes a stop at the Zaharakos Ice Cream Parlor, a historic eatery with a beautifully restored interior.

ADDITIONAL TRAVEL TIPS

Best Time to Visit: Fall (September-November) is the ideal time to visit. The crisp weather and stunning autumn foliage provide the perfect backdrop for outdoor exploration. Golden hour in the morning and late afternoon offers the best natural light for photography.

Comfortable Footwear: Many of Columbus's landmarks are spread across the city, so prepare for plenty of walking. Opt for comfortable shoes to fully enjoy the architectural walking tours.

Dining with a View: After a day of sightseeing, enjoy a meal at Upland Columbus Pump House, a modern brewery located in a restored historic building along the riverfront. The blend of modern design with industrial heritage provides a relaxing atmosphere for unwinding after your tours.

Book Early for Tours: Both guided tours and entry to landmarks like the Miller House require reservations, particularly during peak seasons. Book your spots at least a week in advance to secure your preferred dates.

Exploring Beyond Downtown: While downtown Columbus is packed with modernist landmarks, don’t miss nearby architectural treasures like Zaharakos Ice Cream Parlor and St. Peter’s Lutheran Church, both offering unique blends of history and modern design.

Planning Your Trip

For more information on tours, accommodations, or to plan your visit, check out the official Columbus Area Visitors Center website for additional resources.
